# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sumika is for everyday Mac users who want the benefits of a capable AI
assistant without sending every conversation to a cloud service or paying for
an expensive recurring subscription. Users should not need a technical
background to understand the product or its everyday value.

## Product Purpose

Sumika is a private, local-first AI assistant for macOS. It supports the
familiar tasks people expect from assistants such as ChatGPT, including
writing, translating, summarizing, researching, and working with their own
files. When users need more than conversation, Sumika can perform reviewable
agent tasks and connect to local apps through MCP servers. Success means making
local AI feel useful and approachable while keeping the user in control of
their data, tools, and spending.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, private, capable.

## Anti-references

Sumika should not present itself as a developer-only tool or lead with dense
technical terminology. Avoid enterprise SaaS language, subscription pressure,
opaque automation, and claims that make privacy sound abstract. Advanced
features may use precise technical language after their everyday benefit is
clear.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with everyday value before implementation details.
2. Make privacy concrete by explaining what stays on the Mac.
3. Reveal advanced capabilities in layers instead of making them a prerequisite.
4. Show that the user remains in control of actions, tools, and network access.
5. Present local AI as a practical alternative to expensive recurring subscriptions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use plain, direct language for introductions and primary product explanations.
Do not assume coding knowledge. Explain specialist terms such as MCP where they
appear, keep paragraphs concise, and preserve a clear heading hierarchy for
scanning and assistive technologies.
